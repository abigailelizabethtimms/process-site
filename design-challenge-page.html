Framing the Design Challenge

What is the problem you are trying to solve?

Sponsoring birthday cakes for children whose parents or guardians cannot afford them and providing employment for special needs adults.

1. Frame it as a design question

How can the website help garner more monetary and volunteer support for the charity's cause?

2. State the ultimate impact you are trying to have

The ultimate impact of the website should be increased donations and orders, so the charity can employ more special needs adults, and sponsor more birthday cakes.

3. What are some possible solutions to your problem?

Clearer organization, distinct hierarchy, clear direction and division of users, succinct navigation bar, organized gallery of high-quality photos, prominently displayed awards, and updated charity information.

4. Write down some contexts and constraints you are facing.

I have not yet visited the store to collect and review print literature, no new information/articles/awards to display, small charity, may be hard to collect information from those who have received services, and there are many groups of users to cater to.

The design question restated.

How do I create clearly distinguished user paths that will attract interest and effectively communicate to the many different groups of users?
